Hedy Lamarr

The woman who co-invented the frequency-hopping technology, a precursor to the secure wi-fi, GPS and Bluetooth.

Here's a time line of Hedy Lamarr's life:

  • 1913 - born on the 9th of November.
  • 1933 - 1937 - married and divorced Fritz Mandl.
  • 1938 - performed in movie Algiers.
  • 1939 - married Gene Markey.
  • 1940 - performed in movie Boom Town with Claudette Colbert.
  • 1941 - married Gene Markey.
  • 1942 - co-invented the wireless “frequency hopping”.
  • 1943 - married John Loder.
  • 1951 - married Teddy Stauffer.
  • 1953 - married W. Howard Lee.
  • 1957 - performed in movie The Story of Mankind as Joan of Arc.
  • 1963 - married Lewis J. Boies.
  • 1966 and 1991 - arrested for shoplifting.
  • 1967 - wrote Ecstasy and Me.
  • 1994 - performed in movie Thats Entertainment! III.
  • 2000 - died on the 19th of January.
